Why Choose a Freeride Lesson with Alpine Ski Academy?

Freeriding isn't just about descending through fresh snow. It's about knowing how to read the terrain, anticipating your line, mastering your technique in changing conditions, and above all, doing it safely. At Alpine Ski Academy ski school, we guide you through every step of your off-piste development with a professional, progressive approach adapted to your level.

Progressive methodology adapted to your level, from initiation to advanced improvement.

Knowledge of Terrain and Key Areas

We know the best lines and conditions according to the season and snow state.

Recommended Safety Equipment

We strongly recommend the use of ARVA, shovel, probe, and ABS airbag backpack as basic safety equipment for any freeride lesson, even within the ski area. The ABS backpack, in particular, can be decisive in case of avalanche. We encourage conscious and responsible decision-making as an essential part of freeride practice.

Frequently Asked Questions about Freeride Lessons in Baqueira

‚ùÑÔ∏è What technical level do I need for freeride with Alpine Ski Academy?

To begin our freeride lessons, you need to ski confidently on red runs, maintaining speed control and executing dynamic parallel turns.

It's not necessary to have skied in fresh snow before, but you do need a solid on-piste technique: good stance, ability to adapt to terrain, and mastery of body centering. If you already ski black runs with fluidity, you can opt for intermediate or advanced level lessons.

üéø Is it compulsory to carry an ARVA, shovel, probe, and ABS airbag backpack?

Although our lessons are exclusively conducted within the ski area, we strongly recommend the use of an ARVA, shovel, probe, and ABS airbag backpack in all freeride sessions.

These elements increase your safety and are part of the standard equipment in unpisted terrain. If you don't have them, we can guide you on renting or purchasing them. Specific training must be done with professional guides.
