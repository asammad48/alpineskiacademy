Why Choose a Freeride Lesson with Alpine Ski Academy?

Freeride is not just about skiing down fresh snow. It's about knowing how to read the terrain, anticipate your line, master your technique in changing conditions, and above all, doing it safely. At Alpine Ski Academy, we guide you at every step of your off-piste evolution with a professional, progressive, and level-adapted approach.

TD3 Instructors Specialized in Freeride

Our instructors hold the highest certification in alpine skiing and have specific experience in freeride.

Controlled and Safe Environment

All lessons take place within the ski domain, in authorized and safe areas.

Step-by-Step Technical Progression

A progressive methodology adapted to your level, from beginner to advanced refinement.

Knowledge of Terrain and Key Areas

We know the best lines and conditions depending on the season and snow state.

Recommended Safety Equipment

We strongly recommend the use of an avalanche beacon, shovel, probe, and ABS backpack as basic safety equipment in any freeride lesson, even within the ski domain. The ABS backpack, in particular, can be decisive in case of an avalanche. We encourage conscious and responsible decision-making as an essential part of freeride practice.

Frequently Asked Questions about Freeride Lessons in Baqueira

‚ùÑÔ∏è What technical level do I need to do freeride with Alpine Ski Academy?

To start our freeride lessons, you need to ski confidently on red slopes, maintaining speed control and executing dynamic parallel turns.

It is not necessary to have skied in fresh snow before, but you do need a solid on-piste technique: good position, ability to adapt to the terrain, and mastery of body centering. If you already ski black slopes fluently, you can opt for intermediate or advanced level classes.

üéø Is it mandatory to bring a beacon, shovel, probe, and ABS backpack?

Although our lessons are exclusively within the ski domain, we strongly recommend the use of a beacon, shovel, probe, and ABS backpack in all freeride sessions.

These items increase your safety and are part of the standard equipment in ungroomed terrain. If you don't have them, we can guide you on renting or buying them. Specific training should be done with professional guides.
