How to Get to Baqueira Beret: Practical Routes for Your Ski Trip

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. How to Get to Baqueira Beret

Dreaming of your ski vacation in Baqueira Beret? Of course you are! But for the dream to start well, the first thing is to get there. As local experts in the Aran Valley, we know that planning the trip is crucial. Here you will find all the routes and tips so that your arrival is as pleasant as your stay on the snow.

1. By car: the most flexible option

Traveling by car gives you total freedom to explore the Aran Valley at your own pace. However, not all routes are the same. Knowing the pros and cons of each is key to avoiding surprises, especially in winter.

Route 1: From Central and Eastern Catalonia (Bonaigua Pass)

For whom: ideal if you are coming from central Catalonia, the Maresme, Gerona, or even Barcelona.

Pros: scenic route, with high mountain landscapes and saving kilometers if the conditions are good.
Cons: the C-28 mountain pass can be a challenge in winter; chains are mandatory and there is a possibility of closure due to snow or ice.

Curiosity: when crossing the pass you will see the iconic Bonaigua Cross, a natural viewpoint with spectacular views.

Frequently asked questions about how to get to Baqueira Beret

Q: What is the best route in winter?

A: The safest is the Viella Tunnel (N-230). The Bonaigua Pass is shorter and more scenic, but it depends on the road conditions.

Q: Which airport is closest?

A: The closest is Toulouse-Blagnac (TLS), 2.5â€“3 hours by car. Barcelona-El Prat (BCN) is also widely used.

Q: Can you get there by train?

A: There is no station in Baqueira. The best option is AVE to Lleida-Pirineus and complete the trip with a car, bus, or transfer.

Q: Is it necessary to bring chains?

A: Yes, especially on the Bonaigua Pass and secondary roads.
