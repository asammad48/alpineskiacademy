Baqueira: the perfect progression to learn step by step

The Baqueira 1800 sector is probably the most complete for a beginner, with a very well-marked progression that allows you to consolidate your technique step by step.

Progression in Baqueira

  1. Beginner magic carpet behind the daycare: an almost flat place, perfect for the first hour of skiing.
  2. Carreretes 1 and 2 magic carpets: very easy green slopes where you can start practicing the first wedge turns.
  3. Rabada drag lift: here you work on the advanced wedge and the beginning of the parallel.
  4. Era Cabana (4-seater detachable chairlift): difficult green slope and very easy blue slope, ideal for consolidating the parallel on longer and more varied routes.

Beret: spaciousness, comfort, and reduced lift pass for beginners

The Beret sector is highly valued by beginners for its spaciousness, good visibility, and lower crowds.

Progression in Beret

  1. Beginner magic carpet: wide, flat area, perfect for the first contact.
  2. Beginner T-bar lift: gives access to a long, wide, and very comfortable green slope for linking turns and gaining confidence.
  3. Clot der Os (4-seater detachable chairlift): takes you to higher altitudes, but without a teacher who knows the route well, you could end up on slopes not suitable for your level.

In addition, Beret offers a reduced beginner lift pass, ideal for your first day if you are not yet going to explore the rest of the resort.

Bonaigua: ideal for the first hours

The Bonaigua sector has a very comfortable start for beginners:

Progression in Bonaigua

  1. Beginner rope tow: perfect for the first wedge supports.
  2. Two-seater chairlift (intermediate stop): very short and simple green slope, ideal for the first two hours of learning.

From here, the progression becomes more complicated: the next option is Argulls (an easy but long blue slope with traffic from faster skiers). This makes it less suitable for a beginner on their third day, compared to the much more logical progression of Baqueira and Beret.

Expert tips: common mistakes and how to avoid them

  • Not starting with a teacher: the first steps are the foundation of your future as a skier. A bad start creates blocks, fears, and bad habits that are very difficult to correct later.
  • Skipping progressions too soon: going up to a blue slope without mastering the advanced wedge is one of the most common causes of scares and frustrations.
  • Choosing the wrong time slot: if you only have 2â€“3 hours or want to avoid crowds, we recommend starting classes from 1:45 p.m. From 1:30 p.m. there is a reduced lift pass and the slopes are much emptier.
  • Snow conditions: each sector and each time of day offer different qualities (harder in the morning, softer at midday). At Alpine Ski Academy, we always guide our students to the best area at all times.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Where to rent ski equipment?

In Beret, there is a very convenient rental shop right at the foot of the slopes. For more options in Ruda and Vielha, check our article on ski equipment rental in Baqueira.

Is it better to start in a group or in a private lesson?

A private lesson accelerates learning, avoids fears, and is 100% adapted to your level. For beginners, it is the best investment. Discover more in our article on how to learn to ski in Baqueira.

Conclusion: start your learning safely

Each sector has its charm, but the logical and safe progression is found in Baqueira and Beret. Knowing the right lifts and changing slopes at the right time is key to making your experience fun and without blocks.

At Alpine Ski Academy, we are experts in guiding you through each step of that path. With us, you will learn not only the technique but also how to move around the resort with confidence and safety.
