Freestyle Lessons for All Levels

From the First Switch to Corks

At Alpine Ski Academy, we offer freestyle lessons adapted to your level and goals. Whether it's your first time in the snowpark or you already control the air and want to perfect more technical tricks, we design a personalized and safe progression. It's not about launching without control, but about building a solid foundation to fly with style, awareness, and safety.

üü¢ Freestyle Initiation

First Jumps, Switch, and Basic Features

Ideal for riders who ski well in parallel on red slopes and are curious about freestyle. You will learn the technical fundamentals: switch (skiing backward with control), small jumps, ollies, nose/tail presses, and the first easy rails and features of the snowpark.

Minimum requirements:

Full control in parallel on red slopes and safety in all conditions.

Recommended equipment:

Twin-tip skis, centered bindings, mandatory helmet, back protector, and optional protections.

üîµ Intermediate Level

Aerial Control, Grabs, and Stable Rails

For those who have already tried the snowpark and want to consolidate their technique and style. We will work on stability in small and medium jumps, coordination in the air, grabs (safety, mute, tail), stable sliding on rails and boxes, control in switch, and physical and mental preparation to progress safely to more technical tricks.

Objective:

To gain flow, aerial control, and safety in landings.

Methodology:

Video corrections, specific drills, and detailed analysis of your execution.

Corks, Rotations, and Own Style

For riders who already master the park and want to improve their rotations (360¬∞, 540¬∞, 720¬∞...), introduce inverted tricks (flips/corks), link features, and polish their style. We use video analysis, real-time correction, and detailed technical preparation.

Includes: Impulse techniques, take-off positioning, line correction, and body control in rotations.

Specific preparation available: If you are looking to compete or be part of a club or project, we create a custom plan with you.

Frequently Asked Questions about Freestyle Lessons in Baqueira

‚ùÑÔ∏è What is the minimum technical level required to start freestyle safely?

To start freestyle safely in Baqueira, it is essential that you ski fluently in parallel on red slopes.

This ensures you have the necessary control, balance, and speed management to move around the snowpark, ski switch, perform stable transitions, and handle landings. It is not necessary to have jumped before, but you must master basic alpine skiing to be able to focus on technical learning without unnecessary risks.

üéø What equipment do I need for my freestyle lessons?

You need twin-tip skis with bindings mounted in the center of the ski (optional but advisable), as they allow you to ski switch and perform rotations with greater control.

A helmet is mandatory. We also recommend a back protector, tailbone protection (padded shorts), and specific goggles or mask. If you do not have the material, we can advise you in Baqueira on renting or buying what you need according to your level and goals.
