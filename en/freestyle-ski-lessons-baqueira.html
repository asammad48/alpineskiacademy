Freestyle Lessons for All Levels

From Your First Switch to Corks

At Alpine Ski Academy, we offer freestyle lessons tailored to your level and objectives. Whether it's your first contact with the snowpark or you already control the air and want to perfect more technical tricks, we design a personalised and safe progression. Here, it's not about launching yourself without control, but about building a solid foundation to fly with style, awareness, and safety.

üü¢ Freestyle Initiation

First Jumps, Switch and Basic Features

Ideal for riders who ski well parallel on red runs and are curious about freestyle. You'll learn the technical fundamentals: switch (skiing backwards with control), small jumps, ollies, nose/tail press, first rails, and easy snowpark features.

Minimum requirements:

Full control in parallel on red runs and confidence in all conditions.

Recommended equipment:

Twin-tip skis, centred bindings, compulsory helmet, back protector and optional protections.

üîµ Intermediate Level

Air Control, Grabs and Stable Rails

For those who have already tried the snowpark and want to consolidate their technique and style. We will work on stability in small and medium jumps, air coordination, grabs (safety, mute, tail), stable sliding on rails and boxes, control in switch, and physical and mental preparation to safely progress towards more technical tricks.

Objective:

Gain flow, air control and confidence in landings.

Methodology:

Video corrections, specific drills and detailed analysis of your execution.

Corks, Rotations and Personal Style

For riders who already master the park and seek to improve their rotations (360¬∞, 540¬∞, 720¬∞...), introduce inverted tricks (flips/corks), link features, and refine their style. We use video analysis, real-time correction, and detailed technical preparation.

Includes: Impulse techniques, take-off positioning, trajectory correction, and body control in rotations.

Specific preparation available: If you're looking to compete or join a club or project, we'll create a tailored plan with you.

Frequently Asked Questions About Freestyle Lessons in Baqueira

‚ùÑÔ∏è What minimum technical level do I need to start freestyle safely?

To safely start freestyle in Baqueira, it's essential that you can ski fluently parallel on red runs.

This ensures you have the necessary control, balance, and speed management to move around the snowpark, ski switch, perform stable transitions, and manage landings. You don't need to have jumped before, but you do need to master basic alpine skiing to focus on technical learning without unnecessary risks.

üéø What equipment do I need for my freestyle lessons?

You need twin-tip skis (double-tipped) with bindings mounted centrally on the ski (optional but advisable), as they allow for skiing switch and performing rotations with greater control.

A helmet is compulsory. We also recommend a back protector, tailbone protection, and specific goggles or glasses. If you don't have the material, we can advise you in Baqueira on renting or buying what you need according to your level and objectives.
